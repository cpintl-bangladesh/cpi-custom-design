# CPI Visual Design Philosophy

**"Dignified Presence"**

---

## Manifesto

CPI's visual language is grounded in a philosophy of **Dignified Presence** — a belief that communities at the margins deserve communication that honors their agency, complexity, and humanity. Every design element carries moral weight. The choice of a color, the placement of a photograph, the weight of a typeface — each is a small declaration about who is centered and who is valued.

 **Space and Form** : The layouts breathe. Generous whitespace is not emptiness — it is respect. It says: this person's story deserves room. Rectangle forms anchor content without aggression; rounded corners signal care rather than authority. The CPI leaf motif recurs as a living thing — organic, growing, never static.

 **Color and Material** : CPI Purple carries the gravity of deep institutional trust — a color that has aged into wisdom. CPI Red interrupts that solemnity with urgency and life, like a heartbeat reminder that the mission is alive and immediate. Together they frame a brand that is serious without being cold, passionate without being reckless. Photography always dominates over abstraction — real faces, real contexts, real dignity.

 **Scale and Rhythm** : Large photography anchors the page and places the community at the center. Typography scales confidently — headlines are bold and certain; body text is quiet and respectful. Statistical callouts break the flow with intentional drama: a number that says *this many lives changed* deserves to be felt before it is processed.

 **Visual Hierarchy** : The CPI logo is small and consistent — a signature, not a billboard. The content — the community, the story, the data — dominates. All design decisions subordinate the organization to the mission. This is the mark of an ethical communicator.

 **Expert Craftsmanship** : Every CPI document should feel like it was labored over with deep care, as if a senior designer spent days refining each margin and color interaction. Nothing is accidental. Every rule, every photograph crop, every callout box is the product of painstaking expertise that has been crystallized into these guidelines.

# Zarish Health Design Philosophy

**"Clinical Clarity"**

---

## Manifesto

ZarishHealth's visual philosophy is **Clinical Clarity** — a system designed to serve health workers under pressure, on underpowered devices, in field conditions that demand immediate information without cognitive friction. Every pixel either serves the clinician or it should not exist.

 **Space and Form** : Forms are clean fields for information. Navigation is never ambiguous. Cards have left-border color coding so program affiliation is decoded before reading. All interactive elements are large enough for hands that have been working all day. The offline indicator is always visible — not a shame, but a badge of resilience.

 **Color and Material** : Teal is the anchor — steady, trustworthy, the color of purposeful calm. It was chosen because it is neither the blue of corporate tech nor the green of pharmacy chains — it is distinct, clinical, and coastal (echoing the Cox's Bazar environment). Program colors are warm and human: Mimosa for HCV (warmth, care), Salmon for CVD (body, vitality), Lavender for MHPSS (gentleness, safety). The dark mode (Gunmetal) is for data dashboards where information density requires maximum contrast.

 **Scale and Rhythm** : Patient data renders at generous scale. Nothing smaller than 14px on mobile. Numbers for statistics are large and immediately readable — data is the content, not the decoration. Navigation labels are concise, clinical, bilingual.

 **Visual Hierarchy** : The patient is the primary entity — their name, ID, and status are always prominent. Program and site context is secondary. Administrative metadata is tertiary. The hierarchy mirrors how a clinician actually reads: WHO is this patient → WHAT program → WHAT action is needed.

 **Expert Craftsmanship** : A health platform that feels cheaply made undermines clinical trust. ZarishHealth's design should feel like enterprise-grade software built with humanitarian intent — the precision of a medical device married to the warmth of a community health worker.
