# YPSA Visual Design Philosophy

**"Solidarity in Motion"**

---

## Manifesto

YPSA's visual language embodies **Solidarity in Motion** — a recognition that community change happens through collective action, sustained over time. The deep navy is stability; the white is possibility; the gold is the moment of impact.

**Space and Form** : Navy anchors every composition. It is the visual equivalent of a firm handshake — reliable, grounded, serious. White space provides the oxygen for ideas to breathe. The three connected figures in the YPSA logo are not merely decorative — they are the structural metaphor for every layout: people bound by common purpose.

**Color** : Navy dominates, unchallenged. The only color permitted to interrupt is gold — used sparingly, intentionally, for the single most important number or message. This restraint is not limitation; it is focus. White reverses on navy create the clearest possible contrast for Bengali and English text.

**Typography** : Bengali stands equal to English — never subordinate. Where English goes bold, Bengali goes bold. Noto Sans Bengali carries the weight of the national language alongside Open Sans as the universal connector. Type sizes are generous for field readability.

**Scale** : The community is the protagonist. Individual stories anchor reports. Statistics serve as evidence of collective impact, not display flourishes.

**Expert Craftsmanship** : Clean execution signals professional respect for the communities served. Every document should feel like it came from an organization that takes its work seriously — no flimsy productions, no corners cut.
